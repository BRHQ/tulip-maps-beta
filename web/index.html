For the curious

What's a tulip diagram?

It's the little picture in a rally roadbook that tells a crew what a junction looks like and which way to go — a stem for the road you're on, a bloom for the turn. Rally people just call them tulips. One glance and you know: third exit, then it bends right.

They're read at speed, on a bumpy road, in one look — so they have to be clear and consistent. That's the whole job of Tulip Maps: draw them fast, keep them consistent, and print a roadbook a crew can actually trust.
