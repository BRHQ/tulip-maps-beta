What it is

A roadbook maker for people who run rallies.

Lay your route on the map, drop the controls and waypoints, and Tulip Maps draws the tulip diagrams — the little junction pictures a crew navigates by. Built for regularity rallies and classic-car tours, but honestly good for any route worth writing down. Made by Virtual Marshal, and used on real events.

How it works

Three steps, no manual.

Plan the route

Draw it on the map or let it follow the roads. Drop your controls, waypoints and notes exactly where they belong — town and street names fill in for you.

Draw the tulips

Each junction becomes a tulip — quick guided ones or hand-drawn, sharp or curvy, with side roads, roundabouts and the official road signs. A whole library to reuse.

Check & export

Check every tulip against the GPS track to catch a wrong-way one, then export a printable roadbook, a GPX or a .vmr — ready to run, or to drop into a Virtual Marshal event.

For the curious

What's a tulip diagram?

It's the little picture in a rally roadbook that tells a crew what a junction looks like and which way to go — a stem for the road you're on, a bloom for the turn. Rally people just call them tulips. One glance and you know: third exit, then it bends right.

They're read at speed, on a bumpy road, in one look — so they have to be clear and consistent. That's the whole job of Tulip Maps: draw them fast, keep them consistent, and print a roadbook a crew can actually trust.
